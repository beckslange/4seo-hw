A croissant is a buttery, flaky, crescent-shaped pastry that belongs to the category of viennoiserie, a French term for baked goods enriched with ingredients like butter, eggs, and milk, bridging the gap between bread and pastry. Made from a yeast-leavened dough that is laminated (layered with butter) multiple times, croissants have a rich flavor and a distinctive honeycomb interior, inspired by the Austrian kipferl but perfected with French techniques.
